Sally Goodin is the tune fiddlers reach for when they want to show what they can do. It has been a contest staple for over a century, and it still has teeth.

## Where It Comes From

The tune's recorded history starts in 1922, when Eck Robertson walked into a Victor studio in New York and cut two sides. One of them was Sally Goodin. It is often cited as the first commercial country music recording, which makes Sally Goodin one of the oldest tunes on wax in the whole tradition.

Robertson was from Texas, and Sally Goodin is rooted in that style. Texas fiddling tends to be clean and precise, with long driving bow strokes and a love of variation. Eck Robertson played it that way, and the Texas tradition kept it that way.

## Who Plays It Well

Eck Robertson's 1922 recording is the place to start. It is short, a little rough by modern standards, and completely confident.
