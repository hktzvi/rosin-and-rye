Washington County is one of those fiddle tunes that shows up quickly once you start listening to bluegrass fiddle players. It appears in jams, fiddle workshops, and festival parking lots. The tune sits comfortably in the key of A and has become part of the standard bluegrass fiddle repertoire.

**Did Kenny Baker write it**

Unlike many fiddle tunes, Washington County is usually credited to Kenny Baker. Baker recorded the tune and performed it widely, and his version became the one most bluegrass players learn today.

Fiddle tunes rarely appear out of nowhere. Some musicians have noticed similarities between Washington County and an older swing era tune called Washington and Lee Swing. Whether that connection represents a direct influence or simply a shared musical idea is not completely clear.

The safest description is that Kenny Baker either composed or arranged the tune in the form that became widely known among bluegrass musicians. If you want to read more about Baker himself, his biography is here:
