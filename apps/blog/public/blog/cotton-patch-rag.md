Cotton Patch Rag is an original composition by Mark O'Connor. It has the feel of a 1930s jazz-influenced rag, with a syncopated, swinging rhythm that sets it apart from most fiddle tunes in the old-time or bluegrass traditions.

## Mark O'Connor

O'Connor grew up in Seattle and was a child prodigy on both guitar and fiddle. By his early teens he had won the National Fiddle Championship multiple times, and he went on to play with Doc Watson, Chet Atkins, and Stephane Grappelli before moving into classical and orchestral work.

His writing covers a lot of ground. Some of it is concert music, some of it is straight-ahead contest fiddle, and some, like Cotton Patch Rag, lands in a ragtime-flavored corner that does not fit neatly into any category.
