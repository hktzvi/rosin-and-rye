Gold Rush is a two-part bluegrass instrumental in the key of A. It came out of the collaboration between Bill Monroe and fiddler Byron Berline during Berline's stint with the Bluegrass Boys in 1967. Monroe had the idea; Berline fleshed it out and contributed the out-chorus. The result became one of the most played fiddle tunes at bluegrass jams.

## Byron Berline

Berline grew up in Oklahoma and came up in the contest fiddle world before making his way into professional bluegrass. He met Monroe at the Newport Folk Festival in 1965 and joined the Bluegrass Boys in March 1967. His background in contest fiddle gave him a clean, precise technique, and that shows in how Gold Rush sits on the instrument.

After his time with Monroe, Berline went on to play with the Flying Burrito Brothers, the Rolling Stones, and Stephane Grappelli, among others. He later settled in Guthrie, Oklahoma, where he ran a music store and kept playing until his death in 2021.
